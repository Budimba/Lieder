I see thee in the dark night, Upon the yellow spire, O white moon, Pale shadow of desire! What strange, wandering spirit, O moon, ne  au with a thread, Thro' shadows, Dost lead thee overhead? Art the eyes of dark Heaven? Some fallen cherubim That mocks us beneath thy pallid rim? Art thou naught but ne bouder, Set helpless in the sky, That rolleth for evermore on high? Art thou, I could believe it, That ancient iron bell, That soundeth The hour of the lost in hell? Upon thy roving forehead Tonight they read, may be, What age hath Their grim eternity! Doth a worm gnaw and bite thee, When thy round disk and red Grows narrow, curving overhead? By what mask wert thou blinded The other night? hadst thou The shadow, Fate, upon thy brow? Thou camest pale and mournful, Upon my floors to lie, Thy white horns Crost against the sky. Go hence, O moon that dieth, The form of fair Phœbe Hath fallen Into the silent sea! Thou, thou art the face only, Already worn and lin'd, Thou hidest thyself some cloud behind! Like a bear that is chained, Beneath thine azure eyes The mountains of ocean fall and rise Alway!
